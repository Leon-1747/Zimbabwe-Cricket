Learn About Cricket

Basic Knowledge of Cricket

  • Objective: Score more runs than the opposing team.
  • Teams: Each team has 11 players.
  • Innings: Each team bats and bowls once in a match.
  • Runs: Scored by hitting the ball and running between the wickets.
  • Overs: A set of six balls bowled by a single bowler.
  • Wickets: The batting team loses a player if a wicket or bells fall.
  • Stumps: This is where the bells sit and what the bowlers aim to hit.
  • Bells: These are placed on top of the stumps.

Cricket Rules and Regulations

  • Wides: A ball bowled too far from the batsman for them to hit, which adds one extra run to the batting team.
  • Leg Byes: Runs scored when the ball hits the batsman’s body (not the bat) and goes to the boundary or allows for runs to be run.
  • No Balls: An illegal delivery (like overstepping the crease), which gives a run to the batting team and an extra ball.
  • Dead Balls: A ball that is no longer in play, often due to no action possible, like after a dismissal.
  • Bowling: Bowlers deliver six legal balls per over.They can bowl 5 times in a T20I match and 10 times in an ODI match
  • Batting: Batsmen aim to score runs and defend their wicket.
  • Fielding: Fielders try to prevent runs and dismiss batsmen.
  • Dismissals: Common ways include bowled, caught, and run-out.
  • Types of Matches: Test, ODI (One Day), and T20.
  • Run-Out: When the batsman's stumps are hit before he can make it back to the crease.
  • Crease: Marking on the table that shows the batsman where to stand and the bowler where to bowl from, this is also where stumps are positioned.
  • Table: This is where the crease is drawn and where the bowling and batting takes place.
